## 2. When a Commercial License is Required

You should obtain a commercial license if any of the following apply to your organization:

### A. Corporate Policy Bans (AGPL Compliance)
Many enterprise compliance policies strictly prohibit the use of AGPL-licensed software in internal networks due to risks of code contamination. A commercial license removes all AGPL conditions, allowing compliance teams to approve the deployment.

### B. Proprietary Customizations
If you modify the source code of DMS-O2 (e.g., to build custom connectors, integrate with proprietary ERPs, or modify the user interface) and wish to keep those modifications secret within your organization, you must use a commercial license. Under AGPL-3.0, any modified network service must make its source code available to its users.

### C. White-Labeling & Branding
If you intend to strip DMS-O2 branding, logos, and copyright notices to distribute or run the product under your own brand, a commercial license is required.

### E. OEM Redistribution
If you package DMS-O2 (or parts of its backend, search microservice, or frontend) inside a proprietary hardware appliance or software suite that you distribute or sell to third parties, you cannot use the AGPL version and must obtain a commercial license.

### F. Commercial SaaS Offerings
If you offer DMS-O2 as a hosted Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) tool to external customers for a fee and wish to keep your operational modifications, billing integrations, or proprietary enhancements private, you must operate under a commercial license.
